>> PROJECT_06 / GAME_DEV

TREASURE HUNT

A browser-based 2-level pixel adventure game built in Bitsy — explore dungeons, collect a hidden key, defeat the boss Zan, and claim the legendary treasure.

BitsyPixel ArtBrowser GameNarrative Design

>> PROJECT_OVERVIEW

Treasure Hunt is a browser-based pixel narrative adventure game created in the Bitsy game engine — a minimalist tool for building tiny, story-rich games with pixel art worlds. Designed as a two-level quest, the game puts the player in the role of an adventurer navigating hand-crafted pixel environments in search of legendary treasure, with each level introducing new challenges, characters, and a memorable boss encounter that guards the ultimate prize.

The game opens in Level 1 — a winding dungeon maze filled with hand-drawn pixel art rooms, hidden passages, and dialogue interactions with NPC characters who drop hints about the path ahead. The level's objective is to locate and collect a hidden Key, without which the door to Level 2 cannot be opened. The key is deliberately placed in a non-obvious location, encouraging the player to explore every room and talk to every character — rewarding thoroughness and curiosity. Collecting the key unlocks the transition to the second level and advances the story.

Level 2 escalates dramatically — the environment shifts to a darker, more elaborate dungeon setting as the player approaches the treasure vault. This level introduces Zan, the formidable boss who guards the treasure chamber. Zan is presented through Bitsy's dialogue and interaction system with menacing characterisation — the encounter serves as the narrative and mechanical climax of the game. Defeating Zan (through the game's interaction mechanic) triggers the treasure discovery sequence: the vault opens, the treasure is collected, and the player is rewarded with a victory dialogue and ending scene.

Every visual element in the game is hand-crafted pixel art — the player sprite, NPCs, Zan's boss sprite, dungeon tiles, key item, treasure chest, doors, and environmental decorations were all designed pixel-by-pixel in the Bitsy editor. Bitsy's tile-based room system was used to construct interconnected spaces with deliberate layout to create a sense of exploration and discovery. The game exports as a single standalone HTML file, making it instantly playable in any browser with no installation required.

The two-level progression system and boss encounter design are the creative centrepieces — the key-hunt mechanic in Level 1 drives exploration while Zan's climactic appearance in Level 2 delivers a satisfying narrative payoff, all crafted entirely within Bitsy's intentional minimalist constraints.

>> CHALLENGES

Working within Bitsy's intentional constraints — a limited colour palette, tile-based movement, and simple dialogue scripting — required creative problem-solving to build a game that still feels engaging and complete. Designing the two levels so that the key hunt in Level 1 feels like a natural exploration rather than a frustrating search demanded careful room layout and NPC dialogue placement. Creating Zan as a genuinely threatening presence through only pixel art and text dialogue required writing characterisation that conveyed menace within Bitsy's limited narrative tools.

>> OUTCOME

Treasure Hunt is a complete, playable game that demonstrates the ability to design an engaging experience from scratch — including narrative design, world-building, level progression, and boss encounter design — within the focused constraints of the Bitsy engine. It is instantly playable in any browser, making it uniquely accessible as a portfolio piece.
