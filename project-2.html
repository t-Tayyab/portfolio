ROUGE SHOOTER

A 2D side-scrolling action shooter built in Unity with two full levels, a multi-type enemy AI system, explosive projectile mechanics, a health and points system, and a climactic boss battle.

>> PROJECT_OVERVIEW

Rouge Shooter is a fast-paced 2D side-scrolling action shooter developed in Unity, built entirely in C# with hand-crafted levels, a multi-tier enemy system, and a climactic boss battle. The game challenges players to navigate through two increasingly difficult stages — each filled with diverse enemy types, environmental hazards, and escalating combat — before facing the ultimate boss encounter at the end.

The Player System is fully featured: the character can walk and run at variable speeds, jump with gravity physics, aim and fire projectiles in both directions, and take damage from incoming fire. A dedicated Health System tracks the player's hit points, displayed as a health bar on the HUD. Taking damage reduces health progressively, and reaching zero triggers a game-over state with a restart option. A Points System rewards players for every enemy eliminated, with different enemy types awarding different point values — encouraging aggressive play and rewarding skill.

Level 1 introduces the player to the core mechanics — standard enemies patrol the terrain and fire horizontal projectiles. The player must navigate platforms, eliminate threats, and progress to the level exit. Level 2 dramatically increases the difficulty, introducing new enemy variants, tighter platform layouts, and explosive projectile enemies that launch grenades and arc-fire rockets with area-of-effect damage — forcing the player to move constantly and plan shots carefully.

The Enemy AI System features multiple distinct types: basic ground soldiers who walk patrol routes and fire straight-line bullets, explosive units that lob grenades with blast radius damage, and aerial or elevated enemies that shoot downward at angles. Each enemy type has its own health value, movement speed, fire rate, and detection range — making combat varied and requiring different strategies for each encounter. Enemy behaviours are implemented using Unity's state machine approach in C#, switching between idle, patrol, aggro, and attack states based on player proximity.

The Boss Fight is the game's climax — a multi-phase encounter with a significantly larger enemy that cycles through attack patterns: rapid-fire bullets, explosive barrages, and charge attacks. The boss has a dedicated health bar displayed prominently on screen. Defeating the boss triggers the victory sequence and displays the player's final score.

Rouge Shooter combines procedural enemy AI, multi-phase boss logic, and area-of-effect physics into a fully playable 2D shooter — demonstrating end-to-end Unity game development from concept to polished product.

>> CHALLENGES

The most complex challenge was designing the enemy AI to feel responsive and fair without being frustrating. Explosive enemy projectiles required precise collision detection and blast radius logic — ensuring area-of-effect damage applied correctly to the player while not clipping through geometry. The boss multi-phase system required careful state management in C# to transition between attack modes smoothly based on health thresholds. Balancing the difficulty curve between Level 1 and Level 2 took significant playtesting to ensure the ramp felt earned, not punishing.

>> OUTCOME

Rouge Shooter is a complete, polished 2D shooter that showcases Unity game development skills across physics, AI, game state management, UI systems, and level design. The combination of varied enemy types, a scoring system, and a boss encounter gives the game genuine replay value and demonstrates the ability to take a game from concept to a fully playable product.
